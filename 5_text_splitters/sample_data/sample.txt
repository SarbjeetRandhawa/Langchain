The LangChain framework is an open-source framework designed to simplify the creation of applications using large language models (LLMs). It provides a standard interface for chains, lots of integrations with other tools, and end-to-end chains for common applications.

One of the core components of LangChain is its ability to handle document retrieval. This involves taking large documents, splitting them into smaller, more manageable chunks, and then storing those chunks in a vector database for quick semantic search.

When dealing with large texts, you cannot simply pass the entire document to the LLM due to context window limits. This is where Text Splitters come into play. A Text Splitter breaks down a document into smaller pieces called chunks. The way you split your text heavily impacts the quality of your retrieval. If chunks are too small, they lose context. If they are too large, they might contain irrelevant information or exceed token limits.

There are many ways to split text. The most common is the RecursiveCharacterTextSplitter, which tries to keep paragraphs and sentences together. Semantic chunking, on the other hand, uses embeddings to group sentences that are semantically similar, creating boundaries when the topic changes.
